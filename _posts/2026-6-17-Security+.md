I passed my Security+ exam this month! It's been a while since I've updated this blog, since I've been focusing on studying. The strategy of watching 
the Professor Messer video lectures, quizzing myself with Anki flash cards, and taking practice exams worked out. While preparing, I also completed 
the TryHackMe SOC Level 1 learning path, and got the certificate for completing it. I had considered testing for the associated SAL1 certification, 
but I haven't seen it listed on job postings, and I'm thinking I might just be better off with the extra money in my bank account.

So with that, my cybersecurity degree, and my current level of work experience in tech, I should be at the stage where it makes sense to apply 
to junior level cybersecurity roles. I updated my resume and sent out some applications yesterday. From the looks of things, it seems there are much 
more senior level roles available, but there were still some junior ones that looked suitable for me.

I decided a good general goal is to send more than 10 applications per week. And then if I don't have an offer after 100 applications or by September, it 
will probably be about time to reevaluate my approach. But I think even in that eventuality I should be well-positioned to take an IT help desk role. I'm 
not really in a situation where I need to be in a rush anyway, I'm just really looking forward to my next step in developing my career. And, I think I 
have reason to be optimistic.

I think it would also be a good idea to figure out another homelab project to document here while I wait to hear back from the organizations 
I apply to. That will probably be the next blog post. Unless of course, I get hired sooner than I expected!
