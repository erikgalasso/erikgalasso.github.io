So I spent about a week trying out bug bounty hunting. I signed up on HackerOne and Bugcrowd. I looked through the scope of targets for various programs. I downloaded the community edition of BurpSuite to examine web traffic details, I downloaded FFUF to fuzz for different URLS, and I learned what Google dorking is (though maybe I should have been using Shodan to search?). After some hours of digging, I tried submitting a few reports on the security postures that looked questionable to me. I would like to be able to report back after securing a bounty, but unfortunately the feedback wasn't what I was hoping for.

Of the three reports I submitted, one was marked as Needs More Information for information I didn't have, another was marked as Duplicate with a report from over a year ago that was itself marked Informational, and the other was marked N/A. From what I gather, I needed to have done more to demonstrate true vulnerability, and I think that's a little beyond my capabilities right now. It seems bug bounty hunting is something where you generally need more experience before you're likely to get a decent income from it, and I'm probably better off focusing on my other work for the time being. Still, I think it was a worthwhile learning experience.

My next goal is to prepare myself to apply for a SOC analyst role that seems well suited for me. I think I'll take a few weeks to make sure I hit it out of the park on the application for this one. I know it's not a 100% chance that I'd get it, but I figure even if I'm not hired there, I'll be more ready to take on a similar role elsewhere.
